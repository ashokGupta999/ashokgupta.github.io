Featured Publications

Hunga Eruption Cooled the Southern Hemisphere

Gupta et al., 2025 · Comms Earth & Env

Net radiative cooling of −0.55 ± 0.05 W m⁻² from sulfate aerosols drove a −0.10 K Southern Hemisphere temperature anomaly — contradicting initial warming projections.

Hunga eruption produced measurable cooling, challenging climate projections.

ML-Based Volcanic Umbrella Cloud Detection

Gupta & Bennartz · Manuscript ready

A lightweight ConvLSTM auto-encoder detects umbrella-cloud tops at sub-km resolution with thermal errors below 2 K, capturing 98% of the 2024 Ruang plume evolution.

Automated ML system demonstrates clear value for rapid ash-hazard alerts.

Warm vs. Cold Rain in Continental Storms

Gupta et al., 2023 · Comms Earth & Env

In very warm-based clouds, warm-rain processes account for up to ~80% of surface precipitation, with a strong dependency on cloud-base temperature.

Cloud-base warming shifts precipitation dominantly towards warm-rain processes.

Volcanic Umbrella Clouds: Hunga Tonga

Gupta et al., 2022 · Comms Earth & Env

Satellite observations detected unprecedented umbrella cloud heights (~31 km) and two distinct ice-rich layers, with volumetric flow rates of ~5.0 × 10¹¹ m³ s⁻¹.

Record-high volcanic clouds redefine understanding of eruption impacts.

Ashok Kumar Gupta, Ph.D.

Staff Scientist
Department of Earth & Environmental Sciences, Vanderbilt University

I work at the intersection of aerosols, clouds, climate, and machine learning. My current efforts span deep-learning satellite algorithms for NASA's PolSIR mission, spectrally resolved polar cloud radiative diagnostics using NASA's PREFIRE observations, the role of mineral-dust INPs and secondary ice production in Arctic mixed-phase clouds, reconstruction of the 21st-century global dust cycle, and physics-informed ML for real-time volcanic cloud detection.

Research Areas

  • Aerosol–Cloud–Climate Interactions

    How aerosols govern cloud microphysics, precipitation pathways, and Earth's radiative balance via satellite observations, radiative transfer modeling, and cloud-resolving simulations.

  • Arctic Mixed-Phase Clouds, INPs & Secondary Ice Production

    Mineral-dust and biological ice-nucleating particles (INPs) and SIP mechanisms — rime splintering, ice–ice collisional breakup, freezing-drop fragmentation, sublimation breakup — controlling liquid–ice partitioning in high-latitude mixed-phase clouds.

  • Polar Cloud Radiative Effects & NASA PREFIRE

    Leveraging PREFIRE far-IR/IR spectral observations (~5–54 µm) to characterize spectrally resolved longwave cloud radiative effects across polar regions and evaluate reanalyses in measurement space.

  • Global Dust Cycle & Land–Atmosphere Feedbacks

    Reconstructing the 21st-century dust cycle via multi-sensor observations and inverse modeling; examining how land-use change, soil moisture, and vegetation modulate dust emissions and climate feedbacks.

  • Volcanic Impacts on Climate

    How stratospheric injections of water vapor and SO₂ from eruptions perturb radiative forcing and surface temperatures — including the landmark 2022 Hunga Tonga eruption studies.

  • Deep Learning for Satellite Retrievals

    Deep-learning algorithms for ice-cloud microphysical retrievals (NASA PolSIR) and physics-informed ML for near-real-time volcanic umbrella cloud detection from geostationary imagery.
